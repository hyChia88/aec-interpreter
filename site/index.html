The problem

Why this is hard — and why a black box plateaus

The final step is choosing one element from a retrieved candidate pool that already contains the answer (median 76 candidates, ground truth in-pool 100%). The difficulty is not retrieval but discrimination among visually identical siblings: a façade is a row of indistinguishable windows; a floor is a run of identical doors. Three forces defeat the obvious end-to-end matcher:

  • Identical pixels. What separates two windows is not in either window's pixels — it is in the building's relational structure.
  • An image→graph gap. The evidence is an image; the answer (a GUID) is a graph node. A model that grounds perfectly in the image still stops at the image boundary.
  • Cold start. A new project has the BIM model but no paired site-imagery labels to learn discrimination from.

A fine-tuned multimodal reranker — exactly the end-to-end configuration — reaches just Top-1 6.7% with the answer in the pool, because it must reconstruct the relational signal internally and unreliably. The contribution makes that signal explicit.

Method

One contribution: a type-conditional spatial address, calibrated into a decision

Grounding reduces to recovering a spatial address — a class-specific, image-recoverable relational key that is computable from the IFC model and estimable from the image — then consuming it through a per-field {value, confidence, source} contract with a calibrated, recall-safe rerank that knows when to abstain.

The depth of the idea

Three questions, one address

RQ1 · Representation

What is the minimal sufficient address?

It is type-conditional. A coarse ontological prefix (storey + class) is necessary but saturated — it can't separate same-class siblings. The discriminator is a class-specific topological body: a position-slot (i, M) for an opening ("i-th of M along the host wall"), a connectivity fingerprint for a wall.

oracle Top-1 4.9% → 78.5% · fillers 91% / walls 64%
RQ2 · Mechanism

How to use a noisy address without losing recall?

Hard filtering destroys recall, so the address is a soft prior in a recall-fixed pool. One real extractor closes most of the gap; its confidence passes an ECE calibration gate (AUROC 0.80). Reweighting is a no-op — the payoff is selective prediction: defer the unsure cases, return candidates instead of a confident wrong GUID.

realized 6.6% → 67.6% · defer 20% → 80.6%
RQ3 · Architecture

Where should the relational context live?

A depth law. Deeper context is informationally more unique, but its recovery from an image collapses with depth (per-hop reliability 0.40 → 0.05 → 0). So realizable discrimination saturates at one hop — compile depth into the node, extract at depth ≤ 1.

median |C| 13 → 8.2 (1 hop) → 8.1 → 8.1

It answers when sure — and defers when not

DEFER  AP_SK_092 — predicted slot 1 of 10 but truth is 8 of 10 (wrong); confidence 0.05 is below τ, so the system abstains and returns candidates rather than a confident mistake. This is the triage value proposition.
Honest scope

What we claim — and what we don't

  • Diagnostic ceilings are oracle. RQ1 and the oracle rows assume perfect extraction; the realizable numbers come from one deterministic extractor (the position-slot). The wall fingerprint and remaining descriptors are demonstrated at oracle level — full realization is future work.
  • One synthetic project. Measurements are on a single cold-start IFC model. The form of the address (type-conditional, shallow, image-recoverable, calibrated-soft) is general; specific reliabilities are project-specific.
  • Not a leaderboard claim. We do not claim to beat an end-to-end matcher in the large-data limit. We claim that in the cold-start regime we can measure, the structured address is what makes grounding realizable, auditable, and transferable.
